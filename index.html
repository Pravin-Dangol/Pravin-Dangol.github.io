Control and motion planning of a bipedal robot: Harpy.

[video]
In this project, a planar dynamic model was developed for a biped equipped with thrusters, the gaits were designed through a Hybrid Zero Dynamics approach, while a combination of feedback linearization and nonlinear MPC was used as control schemes.
The control architecture and low-level communication were developed in Simulink (Matlab/C++) and deployed on a prototype operating on an RTOS.

TD3 policy for online trajectory update on a quadruped

In a quadruped, joint level control alone is not robust to perturbation such as push disturbance. In order to promote robustness without relying on computationally expensive online optimization, an RL agent is trained offline to update parameterized trajectories in a trotting gait. The reward in this case enforced stability in the orientation of robot, the policy is updated through a TD3 algorithm.
As a result the agent is able to withstand momentary push disturbance of up to 30% of its total weight.

Compression of a deep hand gesture detection network through sparsity promotion

[code]

This project involved pruning of a large hand pose detection model (InterNet, Dataset: InterHand2.6M). The goal was to compress as much of the CNN layers as possible while keeping accuracy close to the original, the resulting is then expected to run inferece pass faster than the original models. In the end a compression rate of 1.7 was acheived with accuracy loss 0.5% on handedness and degradation of +26mm on MRRPE.
